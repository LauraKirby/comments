Christine5 was having a so/so day

Navarre, FL

I took mom to the doctor today. She was there for just a regular check up to see how she is doing. They gave her that memory test and she scored a 12. Six months ago she scored an 18. That is a drop of 6 points in 6 months. I wasn't sure what that meant so I looked it up and found this:

The maximum MMSE score is 30 points. A score of 20 to 24 suggests mild dementia, 13 to 20 suggests moderate dementia, and less than 12 indicates severe dementia. On average, the MMSE score of a person with Alzheimer's declines about two to four points each year.

I talked to her doctor about adding Razadyne (she takes Namenda XR) because I heard good things about it. Her doctor said that Razadyne comes from the same "family" of medicine as Exalon So it would be ok to add it to see if it helps. Mom had to go off the Exalon patch because she lost her appetite and lost a lot of weight The doctor said that may happen again but it is worth a try. If she tolerates it better than the exalon patch it may help her for a while.

I hate this disease! Hugs to all of you!

EiEi was having a good day

Elmira, NY

It will be 7 months since my husband has been in the nursing facility. I visit almost every day. It seems like it is getting harder each day. He is physically ok, but mind is gone. He looks great and walks the halls all day until he sometimes sits down when he is so exhausted. He has no attention span and is not interested in anything. He knows me, but it is very straining to try and visit with him.I feel so bad for him and the fact that this is the rest of his life! He will be 79 next week and I am so sad and lonely. It is so hard to see him like this. I wish I could come to terms with the situation, knowing he is taken care of, but I just can't. I don't feel I can enjoy anything, knowing what his life is like and how awful it must be. Everyone loves him and he loves all his staff, but I still am not coping with the sadness of it all. Not being able to carry on a conversation with him is heartbreaking. This disease is the worst!

BeverleyMorrison was having a so/so day

Sydney, AU

Well, one of those days when you start out totally in control and before you can say "Jack Robinson" all your plans have gone out the window." Decided I should take my boy to the doctor for his vitamin B shot, had to get some more prescriptions as well. In doing the Vitamin B, phone rings, its the man to fix the blinds, he'd like to come in an hour, tell him to make it two. We have go see the doctor, he orders an exray because he thinks my boys gall bladder is his problem, down the road for the exray, we have to g back Thursday for a scan, of course by now I'm running totally late, get home, the man fixing the pool pump is there and wants to talk about The problem, man arrived to fix the blinds. By the time I got them all out the door, it was 3pm, no lunch, the boy was exhausted and so was I. Roll on tomorrow at least at this stage it's only the chiropractor. What fascinating lives we live?
Roll on tomorrow!
Hugs all
